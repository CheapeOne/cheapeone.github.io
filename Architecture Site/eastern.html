The Eastern Garden 土



This area features a large space of grass and bamboo, surrounding a series of reflection ponds. This section is the largest due to the expanse of green, but also contains the fewest buildings. A few isolated pavilions dot the landscape, making this the most secluded area of the garden.

Looking-Far-Away Pavilion



This pavilion is extremely minimalistic, little more than four columns and a pagoda style roof. This area of the garden is covered in more greenery than any other, so this tiny pavilion does little to take attention away from it.

Celestial Spring Tower




This tower is one of the few buildings on this side of the garden, and is placed at the intersection of a few of its paths. The layout of this building therefore encourages being approached from any direction. This makes sense, as it acts as a housing for a well, hence the name. Being a source of water, it naturally needs to be used often to maintain the plants within the garden. Having an open, octagonal shape allows one to carry water off towards any part of the site.
